The Architecture of Food That Cannot Touch

We called it "picky eating" because our language for childhood distress is often coarse and insufficient.

For years, I watched my son navigate the daily, invisible friction of sensory mismatch. To the outside world, his behavior looked like defiance. To my trained eyes as a nurse, it was a profound physiological protest. He was reacting to the tactile betrayal of clothing textures and the terrifying architectural rigidity of food that could not touch.

When my own late-in-life autism diagnosis arrived, it was the final, missing ingredient. It gave a name to a lifetime of feeling profoundly out of place. I didn't find my path forward in a clinic. I found it in the chemistry and texture of cake.

The 5-Layer Sensory Architecture Framework™

Cake is the ultimate Somatic Laboratory. It is a controlled environment where we can safely calibrate color palettes, structural densities, and flavor profiles to perfectly mirror an internal state.

1. Foundation

Clearing the room to establish a sensory-safe baseline and remove immediate environmental friction.

2. Infusion

Integrating comfort cues and safe, calibrated flavor profiles that soothe rather than overstimulate.

3. Strength

Understanding mouthfeel, density, and tactile feedback to build trust in the kitchen.

4. Strategy

Clearing the mind and mapping out practical, neuro-inclusive steps for daily food interaction.

5. Expression

Finding your anchor by giving tangible, non-verbal shape to internal feelings through design.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this medical therapy or occupational therapy?

No. While I am a former nurse, I do not diagnose or provide medical therapy. I provide Somatic Kitchen Audits to help you translate environmental friction into actionable, creative kitchen practices.

Do we actually bake the cake during the 60-minute audit?

No, the audit is a conversation and a strategy session via Zoom. I evaluate your child's sensory profile and deliver a custom-designed Somatic Cake Recipe for you to execute together in your own time.

My child won't eat cake. Is this still for us?

Absolutely. In Somatic Baking, the goal isn't always consumption. Sometimes the goal is simply tolerating the smell of vanilla, touching the texture of flour, or mixing a color. We use cake as a low-stakes laboratory to build tactile trust.

I'm not in Texas. Can I still book a consultation?

Yes! Because the Somatic Kitchen Audits are conducted virtually via Zoom, I can work with neurodivergent families globally.
